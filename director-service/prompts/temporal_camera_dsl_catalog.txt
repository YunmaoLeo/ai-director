Available camera DSL primitives and guidance:

1. `aerial_follow`
- High moving camera that tracks a subject from above.
- Best for helicopter/drone pursuit, tactical tracking, route readability.
- Typical constraints:
  - `vantage: aerial`
  - `height_bias: aerial`
  - `camera_height: 6.0-14.0`
  - `camera_distance: 1.5-5.0`

2. `top_lock`
- Nearly top-down lock on subject or pair.
- Best for surveillance, abstract geometry, race-line readability.
- Typical constraints:
  - `overhead: true`
  - `top_down: true`
  - `camera_height: 7.0-16.0`
  - `overhead_radius: 0.3-2.0`

3. `helicopter_orbit`
- High circular / semi-circular orbit over the subject cluster.
- Best for showcasing momentum and route shape from altitude.
- Typical constraints:
  - `overhead: true`
  - `camera_height: 6.0-14.0`
  - `orbit_arc_degrees: 120-260`

4. `parallel_strafe`
- Side-on tracking that preserves relative speed and duel readability.
- Best for racing, side-by-side comparison, overtakes.
- Typical constraints:
  - `vantage: high_angle` or `grounded`
  - `distance_scale: 0.9-1.4`

5. `lead_chase`
- Camera slightly ahead of the subject looking back or across its path.
- Best for aggressive pursuit and dominance framing.
- Typical constraints:
  - `camera_offset`
  - `look_at_offset`
  - `fov: 50-75`

6. `tail_chase`
- Camera trails behind the subject.
- Best for speed buildup, route commitment, forward momentum.
- Typical constraints:
  - `camera_distance: 2.0-7.0`
  - `fov: 48-72`

7. `geo_reset`
- Wide or elevated reorientation shot.
- Best for restoring geography after aggressive editing.
- Typical constraints:
  - `vantage: high_angle`
  - `camera_height: 3.5-8.0`

8. `apex_orbit`
- Circular emphasis around a climax moment.
- Best for overtakes, near-collisions, commitment moments.
- Typical constraints:
  - `orbit_arc_degrees: 150-320`
  - `fov: 45-68`

9. `impact_push`
- Strong push-in or compression into a decisive moment.
- Best for emotional punctuation and race turning points.
- Typical constraints:
  - `camera_distance: 0.8-3.0`
  - `distance_scale: 0.5-0.95`
  - `fov: 35-58`

10. `retreat_reveal`
- Pull back to reveal more context over time.
- Best for aftermath, scale reveal, route comprehension.
- Typical constraints:
  - `camera_distance: 2.0-8.0`
  - `distance_scale: 1.0-1.8`

11. `sentinel_watch`
- Static or near-static high watchpoint.
- Best for detached observation, strategic overview, calm control.
- Typical constraints:
  - `camera_height: 5.0-14.0`
  - `vantage: high_angle`

12. `ground_skimmer`
- Extremely low camera close to the ground, emphasizing velocity.
- Best for speed sensation and threat.
- Typical constraints:
  - `camera_height: 0.3-1.0`
  - `fov: 60-85`

13. `roofline_shadow`
- High diagonal camera shadowing the subject from offset.
- Best for dramatic tactical pursuit and layered depth.
- Typical constraints:
  - `camera_height: 4.0-10.0`
  - `camera_offset`

14. `intercept_arc`
- Camera cuts across subject motion instead of simply following it.
- Best for dynamic angle contrast and aggressive editorial energy.
- Typical constraints:
  - `orbit_arc_degrees: 90-180`
  - `distance_scale: 0.8-1.3`

15. `pair_lock`
- Camera treats two moving subjects as a coupled unit.
- Best for duels, rivalries, mirrored motion.
- Typical constraints:
  - `vantage: overhead` or `high_angle`
  - `camera_height: 3.0-10.0`

16. `finish_drop`
- Elevated opening that descends into the action or compresses toward the finish.
- Best for finales, endpoint emphasis, route payoff.
- Typical constraints:
  - `height_bias: high`
  - `camera_height: 5.0-12.0`
  - `distance_scale: 0.8-1.4`

Usage notes:
- You may invent new DSL labels if needed, but prefer the catalog when one clearly fits.
- `constraints.dsl` should name the primary camera behavior.
- Use freeform `shot_type` and `movement` labels alongside the DSL; they do not need to match one-to-one.
- Strong shots usually combine:
  - a framing label (`shot_type`)
  - a motion label (`movement`)
  - a DSL primitive (`constraints.dsl`)
  - 2-4 numeric constraints
